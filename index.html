The Complete

Rolex Submariner

A comprehensive reference guide to every major model from 1953 to present. Explore the evolution of the world's most iconic dive watch.

Historical Timeline

The Submariner's journey from pioneering dive tool to horological icon spans over seven decades of continuous refinement and innovation.

1953 - 1954

Reference 6204

The genesis. The first watch to bear the "Submariner" name on its dial, introducing the world to Rolex's revolutionary dive watch concept with 100m water resistance.

Case Size
37-38mm
Movement
Cal. A260
Water Resistance
100m (330ft)
Bezel
Bi-directional

Key Features

  • Slim case profile with small 5.3mm crown
  • Gilt dial with pencil hands and "lollipop" seconds hand
  • Bold bezel design missing markings between 0-15 minutes
  • Revolutionary Twinlock crown with dual O-rings
  • First official Submariner reference
1954 - 1955

Reference 6200 & 6205

Evolution begins. The 6200 introduced the iconic Mercedes hands and larger crown, while the 6205 came in both clean dial and Mercedes hand variants.

Case Size
37-38mm
Movement
Cal. A296
Water Resistance
100-200m
Dial Variants
Multiple

Key Features

  • Ref. 6200: Larger "Big Crown" for easier underwater operation
  • Introduction of the famous Mercedes handset (6200)
  • Ref. 6205: Some with "clean dial" (no Submariner text)
  • Rare 3-6-9 Explorer-style dial variants (6200)
  • Highly sought after by collectors for distinctive design
1955 - 1959

Reference 6536 & 6538

The "James Bond" era. The 6538 became immortalized when Sean Connery wore it in Dr. No and other early Bond films, cementing its legendary status.

Case Size
38mm
Movement
Cal. 1030
Water Resistance
100-200m
Crown
Big Crown (6538)

Key Features

  • Ref. 6536: Small crown with improved Cal. 1030 movement
  • Ref. 6538: Big Crown variant with 8mm winding crown
  • Some 6536 models were COSC certified (6536/1)
  • Featured in multiple James Bond films (Dr. No, From Russia with Love)
  • Extremely collectible with strong provenance
1959 - 1989

Reference 5512 & 5513

Three decades of excellence. The longest-running vintage references, introducing crown guards and defining the modern Submariner aesthetic we know today.

Case Size
40mm
Movement
Cal. 1530/1520
Water Resistance
200m (660ft)
Certification
5512: COSC

Key Features

  • First Submariners with protective crown guards
  • Crown guard evolution: Square, Eagle Beak, Pointed, Modern (1959-1964)
  • Ref. 5512: COSC-certified chronometer movement
  • Ref. 5513: Non-COSC, more affordable, longer production (30 years)
  • Transition from radium to tritium luminous material (1960s)
  • Multiple dial variants: gilt, matte, glossy
  • Military variants (5513 Milsub) extremely rare and valuable
1969 - 1979

Reference 1680

The date arrives. After 16 years, Rolex added a date complication to the Submariner, creating the Submariner Date lineage that continues today.

Case Size
40mm
Movement
Cal. 1575
Water Resistance
200m (660ft)
Date Window
Cyclops lens

Key Features

  • First Submariner with date complication at 3 o'clock
  • Cyclops magnification lens on crystal
  • Early models had red "SUBMARINER" text on dial (Red Sub)
  • Later models featured white text
  • Available in stainless steel only initially
  • Highly collectible, especially red text variants
1979 - 1988

Reference 16800

Modern materials arrive. The transition to sapphire crystal and increased water resistance marked the beginning of the contemporary Submariner era.

Case Size
40mm
Movement
Cal. 3035
Water Resistance
300m (1,000ft)
Crystal
Sapphire

Key Features

  • First Submariner with scratch-resistant sapphire crystal
  • Tripled water resistance to 300m through improved case seals
  • Early models: matte dial with painted indices
  • Later models: glossy dial with applied white gold indices
  • Unidirectional bezel replaced bi-directional (later production)
  • Cal. 3035 introduced quickset date function
  • Transitional reference bridging vintage and modern eras
1988 - 2010

Reference 16610

The modern classic. Over two decades of production made this the most recognizable and beloved Submariner of the contemporary era.

Case Size
40mm
Movement
Cal. 3135
Water Resistance
300m (1,000ft)
Production
22 years

Key Features

  • Powered by legendary Cal. 3135 (still used in variants today)
  • Enhanced accuracy and reliability over Cal. 3035
  • Glossy dial with applied white gold hour markers
  • Unidirectional bezel with aluminum insert
  • Early models: tritium lume, hollow end links, lug holes
  • Later models: Luminova/Super-LumiNova, solid end links, no lug holes
  • Ref. 16610LV "Kermit" (2003): 50th anniversary green bezel
  • Engraved rehaut added circa 2007
2010 - 2020

Reference 116610

Ceramic evolution. The "Super Case" design with Cerachrom bezel brought modern materials and increased robustness to the Submariner.

Case Size
40mm
Movement
Cal. 3135
Water Resistance
300m (1,000ft)
Bezel
Cerachrom

Key Features

  • Virtually scratchproof Cerachrom ceramic bezel insert
  • "Super Case" with thicker lugs and crown guards
  • Maxi dial with larger hour markers and hands
  • Chromalight blue lume for improved visibility
  • Ref. 116610LV "Hulk": green dial and green bezel
  • Glidelock clasp extension system on bracelet
  • Solid center links on Oyster bracelet
2020 - Present

Reference 126610

The current generation. Enhanced movement technology and refined proportions represent the pinnacle of Submariner evolution while honoring its heritage.

Case Size
41mm
Movement
Cal. 3235
Water Resistance
300m (1,000ft)
Power Reserve
70 hours

Key Features

  • New generation Cal. 3235 with extended 70-hour power reserve
  • Slightly larger 41mm case for modern proportions
  • Slimmer lugs despite larger case size
  • Chronergy escapement for improved efficiency
  • Ref. 126610LV: "Starbucks" with black dial, green bezel
  • Ref. 124060: No-date variant continues
  • Enhanced bracelet with refined Glidelock system
  • Improved anti-magnetic properties
